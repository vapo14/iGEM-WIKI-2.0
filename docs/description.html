Inspiration

When we first heard about iGEM, we also heard about the wonders of synthetic biology, and were more than inspired by the projects we read about. Learning about all the things that had been done only reaffirmed the notion that not even the sky was our limit. So what better idea than to use all these incredible tools to solve a real world issue in such a way that we could notice an impact in our own community?

Our team is comprised of people who have lived most of their lives in the state of Chihuahua. Knowing our home and our people for so many years and in so many different aspects, we wanted to do something that would truly influence our community. We wanted to create a meaningful change in our surroundings. With this as our main focus, we launched into our brainstorming sessions. A topic that resurfaced throughout all the wild and countless ideas we considered, was agriculture.

For our country, agriculture is one of the major economic activities. Besides being a leading agent in the job market, it fulfills the nutritional needs of millions of citizens, preserves natural environments, stimulates progress by improving the quality of life in rural areas, and has also been ingrained in our culture and traditions since the very beginning. The Mexican countryside counts itself among the 12 economies with the highest agricultural production on the world; employing 5.5 million people and generating around 27 billion dollars annually. 1

Even before being aware of these impressive statistics, we already knew agriculture had a considerable impact in our country and state. Before being part of iGEM Tec-Chihuahua 2019, some of our team members had the chance to meet and work with people immersed in this area, others had developed small research projects in our school regarding agricultural issues, but all of us had some sort of experience with agriculture and its importance for the Mexican people.

The interest, curiosity, and familiarity with this topic remained a constant throughout the first weeks we spent searching for that special cause we would focus our project on. Once we realized that, we began exploring what issues influenced this area the most so we could try and solve them.

Description

OUR CAUSE

Agriculture has had a major impact in almost any aspect of any civilization. And today it still does. Economically, culturally, socially, this basic human activity influences our lives. Whenever it suffers, people suffer, since its effects echo exponentially. Rising population and consequent increasement in consumption needs, result in a great demand upon agriculture and the resources it provides. Nutritional requirements, economic stability, employment rates, and many other factors rely on agriculture.

Illustrating its importance goes beyond numbers, but we might as well try:

IN OUR WORLD

26.7% of the world’s total population are employed in agriculture.

1 384.8 millions of hectares in the whole world are designated for harvesting.

“In the coming decades, increased investment in agriculture is going to be crucial for meeting the challenge of adequately feeding the 821 million chronically undernourished people counted today.” FAO, 2018 13

IN OUR COUNTRY: MEXICO

Agriculture stands as the main generator of jobs in the whole country. Mexico stands among the 12 countries with highest agricultural production. 13% of our countryside is dedicated to agriculture. It employs 5.5 million people. It generates 27 billion dollars annually. 1, 3, 9, 10

IN OUR STATE: CHIHUAHUA

Our state holds the first place in the national production of more than 10 different crops. Over a million hectares of Chihuahua’s land belong to the agricultural sector. Annually in our state this sector produces over 1.4 million dollars. 2, 3

OUR TARGET: VERTICILLIUM DAHLIAE

Pests, weeds, and diseases are responsible for 30 to 40% of crop losses each year.

Verticillium wilt is one of the most important crop diseases in the whole world. In some cases it accounts for a loss of 20% of crops in several regions, and in a single season it can cause an economic loss of 3 million dollars. It affects more than 400 different plant species, which include cotton, potato, tomato, strawberry, avocado. 4

The disease is caused by a phytopathogenic fungus called Verticillium dahliae. This fungal pathogen is highly resistant to extreme environmental conditions and chemical treatments. It remains in the soil up to 10 years even after crop treatment and has a wide range of hosts as well as numerous defense mechanisms. 4

Here in our state it is especially detrimental to cotton crops, which are one of our main agricultural products. Just last year 170, 000 hectares of Chihuahua’s agricultural land were destinated to cotton production. Worldwide it stands as the 13th place in cotton production, and nationally it’s the 1st. Verticillium wilt results in a 20% cotton loss, making it the most relevant agricultural disease in our state. 11

OUR V-TION

In order to fight this disease, we chose to produce four recombinant proteins through synthetic biology techniques. These proteins are antimicrobial peptides with different action mechanisms that result in an overall antifungal effect.

AtPFN1

Arabidopsis thaliana 12
  • Binds to actin
  • Can prevent the polymerization of actin
  • Affects the cytoskeleton

PsDef1

Pinus sylvestris (Scots pine) 5
  • Inhibits α-amylases of certain pine pests
  • Unknown ability to permeate into the membrane

WAMP1b

Triticum kiharae 7, 8
  • Binds to chitin
  • Causes browning of hyphae and prevents their elongation
  • Results in destruction and discoloration of spores

AhPDF1.1

Arabidopsis halleri 6
  • Causes morphogenic growth inhibition
  • Toxic to fungal cells through an unknown mechanism

OUR PLAN

Plant antimicrobial peptides are known for being small (45-50 amino acids), basic, rich in cysteine, and having 4 or more disulfide bonds. 5, 6, 8, 12

All of these characteristics make their production as recombinant proteins in prokaryotic systems a challenge. But we decided to take it. Seeing this as an opportunity to push our boundaries of knowledge, creativity and resilience, we chose E. coli as our chassis.

What we want to do is difficult, but not impossible. Through extensive research, we came upon a set of approaches we believe will allow us to produce these special peptides successfully in our prokaryotic organism of choice.

In order to achieve this, we chose SHuffle® T7 Express Competent E. coli as our expression vector. This is a new strain, developed by NEB, specially designed to aid in the correct production of disulfide bonds. Using this organism, we will design our own vectors, fitting to our needs and specifications, and produce our 4 recombinant proteins. Once we achieve this, we will purify them, assess their antifungal activity, and use them to create a fungicidal product against this disease.

We believe that through the development of V-TION, an effective, ecofriendly, and accessible biopesticide, fungal diseases that affect crops all over the world, such as Verticillium wilt, can be fought properly.
