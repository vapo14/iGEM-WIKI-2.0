When we first heard about iGEM, we also heard about the wonders of synthetic biology, and were more than inspired by the projects we read about. Learning about all the thing that had been done only reaffirmed the notion that not even the sky was our limit. So, what better idea than to use all these incredible tools to solve a real-world issue in such a way that we could notice and impact in our own community.

Our team is comprised of people who have lived most of their lives in the state of Chihuahua. Knowing our home and our people for so many years and in so many different aspects, we wanted to do something that would truly influence our community. We wanted to create a meaningful change in our surroundings.

Before being part of iGEM Tec-Chihuahua 2019, some our team members had the chance to meet and work with people immersed in the rural area, while others had developed small research projects in our school regarding agronomic issues; somehow all of us had some sort of experience with agriculture and its importance for Mexican people.

Four our country, agriculture fulfills the nutritional needs of millions of citizens, preserves natural environments, stimulates progress by improving the quality of life in rural areas, and has also been ingrained in our culture and traditions since the very beginning.7

A certain concern seemed to stand out among the people our team members knew and had worked with. Both agronomic engineers and crop producers involved in the cotton agricultural business, mentioned Verticillium, a phytopathogenic fungus that wilts their crops and has only worsened with every new season. This particular disease has been on of their major problems for more than 7 years, and still they haven’t received any help about this whatsoever. This year the overall production is expected to lower considerably compared to previous seasons. One of the principal measures agricultors use as an attempt to control this situation is applying great amounts of agrochemicals; representing a hazard regarding soil, water and air pollution.

Unfortunately, so far there is no product or efficient treatment to deal with this fungal infection. Besides, the lack of proper methods to detect this disease in its earliest stages represent another crucial concern.

Due to the above, we do believe that through synthetic biology we can reach a solution to the inhibition of V. dahliae, support of the most important sector of our region by improving the production amount and preserving this valuable product that our state and our country produce and reducing fungicide pollution in consequence of toxic chemical components, in addition to help to decrease the suicide rate because of them.

Description

Verticillium dahliae has a wide range of hosts, among which herbaceous plants and perennial wood are included, affecting more than 300 species, including cotton, strawberries, raspberries, blueberries, potatoes, lettuce, among others; even more importantly, its effects are worldwide.8

China is the world’s second biggest cotton producer and Verticillium wilt’s rates have been increasing in the past decade. V. dahliae, as well as another wilt disease, have had a great impact in the country’s cotton production, losing more than 150,00 tons per year.9

Meanwhile, in the US, cotton wilt causes annual losses around 0.5% and 3.5% of the crops at a national level. In certain states, such as New Mexico, these rates have reached 5% and in Alabama, cropping fields have decreased 1.5% of its extension; these losses translate into roughly 3 million dollars.10

Speaking of Mexico, cotton production is highly important seeing that the amount produced accounts for 80.67& of total national demand; in 2018, cotton production represented a profit over $10,343,005,600 for the country.11 Chihuahua is the biggest cotton producer of Mexico with its yield being 70.2% of all national production.5

Currently, there is not any Verticillium specialized and aimed product, cotton producers turn to the conventional approach. They add agrochemicals to their crops, without any proper instructions and in unawareness of the potential consequences. This elicits unwanted effects, such as elimination non-target organisms, and affectation of the food chain, high risks to the environment and human health. Even, people are being affected too, over 3 million agricultors suffer from serious poisoning, while 25 million suffer from mild poisoning.12

Also, trophic chains have been affected by this. A given example is the bee population reducing 29%-36% due to excessive use of triazoles.13

Another method employed to reduce Verticillium caused infections is crop rotation which consists of alternating plants with different nutritional needs in one place during different cycles, however it can result in disease propagation. Also, a frequent used technique is solarization, it refers to soil disinfestation through the heat generated from the solar energy captured; nevertheless, despite its positive outcomes, demands a great amount of time.14

What a about this fungus?

Verticillium dahliae is stimulated by humid soils and temperatures between 21°C and 27°C (70°F-80°C). This makes tropical and subtropical regions a suitable environment for its growth. The fungus can overwinter as mycelium in perennial hosts, plant debris, and vegetative propagative parts.15

Life cycle and action mechanism

It can be divided into three phases:

Latent phase: as a microsclerotia, made from melanin, Verticillium can survive in the soil from a single year to over two decades. Once roots exudates are found in the surrounding, the microsclerotia starts to germinate and spreads inside the plant through its mycelial form or as spores called microconidia. Once this happens, it travels into the plant’s vascular system, worsening the infection.16

Parasitic phase: the fungus colonizes the plant’s structure, especially its roots and stems, producing in turn more microsclerotia. Infected plant tissue turns necrotic since the vascular system is obstructed with mycelia, conidias, and fungal metabolism byproducts. The pathogen itself produces a glue-like substance that also obstructs the plant’s vessels, provoking water blockage and tissue wilting.17

Saprophytic phase: the plant dies, therefore the microsclerotia gets into the soil and starts with the cycle all over again.18

Usually, Verticillium wilt symptoms vary among hosts, but certain common symptoms can be pinpointed: premature leaf chlorosis, necrosis and vascular discoloration in stems and roots.19

Looking forward to helping our environment and cotton industry, a systemic, preventive and treating biofungicide using recombinant antifungal peptides as active ingredients is sought. So, in order to fulfil our objective, the following methodology was designed with the purpose of turning our project from an idea to reality.

BioBrickTM Desing: a total of five DNA sequences were designed to express recombinantly the peptides in E. coli. The constructs count with the essential parts needed for their correct translation into proteins, purification, and proper folding. Recombinant production of disulfide bonded peptides, like PsDEF1 and WAMP1b, can be complicated in prokaryotic systems, thus we have as well created DNA sequences for the co-expression of these peptides with Erv1p, a disulfide bond forming protein.20

E. coli Cells Transformation: Chemically competent E. coli cells are prepared and transformed with our vectors. E. coli BL21 (DE3) was chosen as a chassis for the expression of AtPFN1 for being ideal for protein expression. In the case of PsDef1 and WAMP1b, E. coli Shuffle, a specially designed strain to aid in the proper formation disulfide bonds, was chosen as an expression vector.

IPTG induced protein expression: After the cells are correctly transformed, expression of the peptides can be induced and controlled by Isopropyl β-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG); BL21 (DE3) and SHuffle are T7 expression strains. Once IPTG is added to these strains, T7 RNA polymerase is produced and capable of transcribing downstream of promoters like those found in the DNA constructs.21

Soluble Protein Extraction: After induction and a period of incubation, cells are lysed through a series of freeze/thaw cycles with the help of a lysis solution. The lysate is then centrifuged to obtain the cells soluble proteins in the supernatant.

Protein Purification: Our peptides are designed to be expressed as fusion proteins with a 6x His-Tag. This allows the peptides to be purified through immobilized metal affinity chromatography, a purification procedure based on the interaction of metal ions with polyhistidine sequences in tagged proteins.22

Antifungal Assay: Next, the antifungal activity of the peptides is tested against V. dahliae. A spore suspension is treated with the purified peptides individually as well as a mixture of all three of them to prove their collaborative effect against the fungus.

Delivery: Nanoencapsulation has been shown to be a promising alternative to deliver active ingredients more efficiently and accurately. Our purpose is to get to nanoencapsulate recombinant peptides, given they are the biofungicide’s active ingredients, using chitosan-poly(lactide) copolymer capsule. 23

What are our peptides?

WAMP1b


WAMP1b is part of the WAMPs antimicrobial peptides, a type of chitinase-like peptides from Triticum kiharae. These antimicrobial peptides are also called hevein-like proteins due to their similarity to hevein and to having a chitin binding site similar to chitinases.24 The peptide’s action mechanism consists in the inhibition of fungalysins, fungal defense metalloproteases. Fungalysins cleave chitinases, plant proteins that degrade chitin found in the fungal cell wal. 25 Since V. dahliae produces fungalysin.26, WAMP1b can be used to inhibit the fungus’ defense system, thus suppressing its pathogenicity.

AtPFN1


AtPFN1 is a profilin protein from Arabidopsis thaliana. These protein bind to chitin, an important mechanism for their antifungal action. It degrades spore and hyphal cell wall, then accumulating in the cytoplasm where it generates reactive oxygen species and induces apoptosis. AtPFN1 has been shown to break down fungal cell walls, even when it is in its dormant, spore phase, ideally preventing the spread of V. dahliae and other fungi.27

PsDef1


PsDef1 stands for Pinus sylvestris Defensin 1, a plant defensin that has been shown to produce morphological changes to fungi mycelium. It presents significant similarities to RsAFP2 ( Raphanus sativus antifungal peptide). RsAFP2, upon binding to GlcCer, produces reactive oxygen species inside the fungal cell, effectively killing it from the inside.28 Additionally, PsDef1 contains 4 disulfide bonds which grant it resilience against environmental changes.29

In such a way that, the peptides’ nature and the delivery properties of the nanocapsule will allow the biofungice to act both as preventive and treating product during planting season.

Once the nanoencapsulation is achieved, the product could be lyophilized and turned into wettable powder (WP). In this presentation it can be applied to the crops through the watering system. After a field research, we realized people involved with the crops knew about new agricultural products through their technical assessor, who learned about them through distributors. Considering this informational chain, our aim is that the final product reaches agrochemical products development company.

Finally, bibliographic and field research were carried out, seeking for the final product to assure the greatest benefit for all parties involved (textile industry or cotton consumers, marketers, cotton crops producers, environment, etc…). Through interviews the producer’s wishes were validated and through detailed consultation of NOMs like NOM-232-SSA1-2009 and NOM-045-SSA1-1993, the proposal of a law-abiding project was sought. Likewise, a through risk analysis was developed, considering any unfavorable situation our project might pose.
