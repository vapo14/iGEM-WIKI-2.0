V-TION

Abstract

Verticillium dahliae is a phytopathogenic fungus that affects more than 300 species around the world, including cotton. It attacks the plant’s vascular system, causing wilting and reducing the product’s quality and overall crop production 2 . Infections by this fungus have caused tremendous losses in the agricultural sector. Around 150,000 tons of cotton, translating into over $8,700,000 1 USD, have been lost every year during recent decades from diseases caused by this particular organism 3 . 

To prevent and inhibit V. dahliae’s infection, the transformation of E. coli to produce the following three antifungal peptides recombinantly, is presented: AtPFN1 has been shown to break down fungal cell walls, even when found as spores 4 ; WAMP1b inhibits fungalysin, which is a fungal enzyme produced as a response to the plant’s own defense mechanisms 5 ; finally, PsDef1 produces morphological changes to fungal mycelium 6 .

Additionally, the elaboration of a systemic, preventive and treating biofungicide using the produced recombinant antifungal peptides as an active ingredient, is proposed.
